Y2K

And Y2K-1, Y2.01K. and Y2K38.

What?

"The Y2K problem is the electronic equivalent of the El Ni√±o and there will be nasty surprises around the globe." - John Hamre, United States Deputy Secretary of Defense

The Y2K Bug was caused by the shortening of 4-digit years (1999) into 2-digit abbreviations (99). This was easily solves but worried many about total tech failure if it wasn't done fast enough.

Y2K38?

The year 2038, when all 32-bit computers will stop working due to too many seconds passing since Jan 1, 1970 (UNIX Timestamps). But by then all computers should be 64-bit, so that shouldn't be a problem.
